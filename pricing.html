Program Structure

The Curriculum

Nine focused modules. One capstone simulation. One certification.

01
Cyber Analyst Foundations
The cognitive framework behind effective analysis. How analysts think, what makes a conclusion defensible, and how to structure uncertainty into actionable assessment.
Core
02
Log Reading and Event Interpretation
State actors, cybercriminal groups, and insider threats. How to build adversary profiles and anticipate behavior based on intent, capability, and opportunity.
Core
03
Alert Triage and Prioritization
Deep-dive into ATT&CK tactics and techniques as operational analysis tools — not memorization lists. Behavior-based detection logic and TTP mapping.
Applied
04
Timeline Analysis and Incident Reconstruction
How a SOC actually functions. Alert prioritization, escalation paths, SIEM fundamentals, and working an alert queue under operational pressure.
Applied
05
Hypothesis Development and Competing Explanations
Reading Windows Event Logs, network logs, endpoint telemetry, and cloud audit trails. Pattern recognition in noisy data environments.
Technical
06
Adversary Behavior and APT Pattern Recognition
NIST IR framework applied. Detection through post-incident reporting. Containment decision-making, evidence preservation, and stakeholder communication.

Adversary OPSEC Evaluation
Hypothesis-driven threat hunting methodologies. Building hunt playbooks, querying data sources, and documenting findings for actionable intelligence.
Advanced
08
Digital Evidence Correlation and Case Building
Writing assessments that communicate clearly under time pressure. Tailoring analysis for different audiences. Confidence calibration and estimative language.
Advanced
09
Communicating Analytic Judgments
Producing intelligence-grade briefings and written assessments. Tailoring findings for technical and executive audiences with precision and clarity.
Advanced
