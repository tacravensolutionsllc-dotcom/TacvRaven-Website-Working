Program Structure

The Curriculum

Nine focused modules. One capstone simulation. One certification.

01
Cyber Analyst Foundations
The cognitive framework behind effective analysis. How analysts think, what makes a conclusion defensible, and how to structure uncertainty into actionable assessment.
Core
02
Log Reading and Event Interpretation
Reading Windows Event Logs, network logs, endpoint telemetry, and cloud audit trails. Pattern recognition in noisy data environments: finding the signal in real log sources.
Core
03
Alert Triage and Prioritization
How a SOC actually functions. Alert classification, escalation paths, SIEM fundamentals, and working an alert queue under operational pressure without letting noise dictate outcomes.
Applied
04
Timeline Analysis and Incident Reconstruction
Sequencing events from raw log and telemetry data to reconstruct what happened, when, and in what order. Building an accurate incident timeline is the foundation of every post-incident report.
Applied
05
Hypothesis Development and Competing Explanations
Structured analytic techniques applied to real-world analysis problems. How to form defensible hypotheses, evaluate competing explanations, and reduce cognitive bias in your conclusions.
Technical
06
Adversary Behavior and APT Pattern Recognition
State actors, cybercriminal groups, and insider threats. How to build adversary profiles and anticipate behavior based on intent, capability, and opportunity using ATT&CK as an operational tool.
Technical
07
Adversary OPSEC Evaluation
How attackers hide their tracks and what that reveals about their intent and capability. Evaluating adversary operational security to identify gaps, pivot points, and attribution indicators.
Advanced
08
Digital Evidence Correlation and Case Building
Connecting artifacts across log sources, endpoint telemetry, and network data to build a coherent, evidence-backed case. How analysts turn raw data points into a defensible investigative conclusion.
Advanced
09
Communicating Analytic Judgments
Producing intelligence-grade briefings and written assessments. Tailoring findings for technical and executive audiences with precision and clarity.
Advanced
Final Assessment

Capstone SOC / CTI Simulation

The TRCIA I certification is not awarded by a written exam. It is earned through a full-scenario simulation requiring you to execute analyst tasks against a realistic threat environment: alert triage, adversary analysis, incident documentation, and final intelligence assessment.
