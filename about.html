Our Story

Why We Started TacRaven

We're United States Military Veterans with backgrounds in intelligence analysis. During our service and in the years since, we've watched the cybersecurity and intelligence fields grow closer together—yet the gap between how these disciplines train, communicate, and operate remains frustratingly wide.

Intelligence professionals bring structured analytical techniques, rigorous source evaluation, and threat assessment methodologies that cybersecurity desperately needs. Meanwhile, cyber practitioners have technical depth that intelligence analysts often lack. The problem? There's nowhere that bridges this gap effectively.

We decided to build that bridge ourselves.

"The best threat intelligence combines the analytical rigor of the intelligence community with the technical depth of cybersecurity. We're building resources that bring both worlds together."

TacRaven Solutions was founded by veterans who believe that quality education shouldn't be gatekept behind expensive paywalls. We're creating resources that make cybersecurity careers accessible—whether you're transitioning from the military, coming from an intelligence background, or starting from scratch. Real knowledge, practical skills, no BS.

Our Philosophy

Bridging Intelligence and Cybersecurity

The intelligence community and cybersecurity field have more in common than most people realize. Threat analysis, pattern recognition, structured analytical techniques, source evaluation—these skills translate directly.

But too often, these disciplines operate in silos. Intelligence professionals don't know how to break into cyber. Cybersecurity practitioners lack the analytical rigor that intelligence training provides. And everyone pays too much for education that doesn't prepare them for real-world challenges.

We're building resources that bridge this gap. Our certification prep tools are free because we remember what it was like to transition out of the military, trying to figure out which certifications actually matter and whether we could afford them.

Our content brings intelligence tradecraft into cybersecurity—structured analysis, threat assessment, and rigorous methodology—while making cyber concepts accessible to those coming from non-technical backgrounds.

What We Believe

These principles guide everything we build:

  • Intelligence tradecraft makes better cyber analysts
  • Veterans deserve a clear path into cybersecurity
  • Education should be accessible, not gatekept
  • Free resources should actually be useful
  • Practical skills beat theoretical knowledge
